You step through the door, leaving the quiet room of books behind—and wake with a start. Your head jerks upright. The library swims into view. You’re back in the chair where it all began, blinking against the late afternoon light slanting through the tall windows. The room is just as it was: shelves towering around you, quiet and still with the faint rustle of nearby pages and the gentle tick of the old clock on the wall.

Had you dozed off? The book lies open in your lap, exactly where you left it… and yet, the pages are turned to a passage you don’t remember reading. You touch the cover. It’s warm.

Your heart is still racing. You close your eyes, just for a moment—and you see him. Rowan. Elara. The grove. You breathe out slowly. It felt so real.

And as you rise to leave, the weight of the book in your hands feels heavier than it should—like it remembers too.
